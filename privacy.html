Privacy

KEP should be useful without collecting more data than needed.

This page explains the privacy direction for KEP before public launch. The final policy should be reviewed before real users are invited.

Privacy-first rule

Learning features should not require sensitive identity information unless it becomes truly necessary.

Do not ask students for Tazkira or sensitive documents for basic learning access.

What KEP may store

  • Account email for login.
  • Profile preferences such as dream field or study goal.
  • Practice and mock attempt history.
  • Saved resources and study plans.

What KEP should avoid

  • Unnecessary identity documents.
  • Private student information sent to AI tools.
  • Publishing personal progress publicly.
  • Sharing user data with volunteers without permission.
AI privacy

AI should help with content, not expose students.

AI automation should be used mostly for reviewing educational content, drafting explanations, and improving study guidance. It should not receive unnecessary personal student data.
