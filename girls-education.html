Dedicated mission space

For the girls who were told to stop learning.

This part of KEP is for Afghan girls whose school doors were closed beyond primary level. It must be private, respectful, low-data, and useful — a quiet space to continue learning dot by dot.

School doors closed for girls beyond Grade 6 for 1537 days

Counter starts from 23 March 2022, when girls’ secondary schools were closed again after a promised reopening.

Why this space exists

KEP should never forget the students who were pushed out of the classroom.

This is not only a feature. It is the soul of the mission: education should remain reachable, even when the classroom is taken away.

🔒

Private learning

Students should be able to learn without exposing unnecessary personal information.

📱

Low-data access

Pages, notes, PDFs, and practice should work well on simple phones and weak internet.

🌱

Hope and confidence

The platform should remind girls that their minds, dreams, and futures still matter.

Girls learning pathway

A gentle study route from foundation to Kankor readiness.

Some students may have missed months or years of formal schooling. This pathway should not shame them. It should help them rebuild step by step.

What this section will include

Foundation lessons

Simple Math, Science, languages, and general knowledge from the basics.

Private practice

Daily questions, saved progress, and wrong-answer review.

Kankor bridge

A bridge from missed schooling toward exam preparation and dream-field paths.
