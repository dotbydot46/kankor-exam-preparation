KEP v5.1.1 • AI automation roadmap

Use AI to help KEP, not to replace human review.

AI can speed up cleaning, checking, translating, explaining, and recommending study plans. But published educational content must still be reviewed by humans.

Safe AI uses Build phases

Golden rule

AI can suggest. KEP reviewers approve. No AI-generated question should become “Published” without human checking.

Never put private AI API keys directly inside frontend HTML/JS files.
Automation opportunities

Where AI can help KEP most

Content cleaner

Fix spelling, spacing, grammar, and option formatting before a reviewer checks the question.

Duplicate detector

Warn reviewers when a new question looks similar to an existing published question.

Explanation helper

Draft simple explanations for answers so students learn from mistakes.

Translation draft

Create first-draft Pashto, Dari, and English versions. Human review remains required.

Difficulty estimate

Suggest Easy, Medium, or Hard based on question wording and student performance.

Study coach

Use weak subjects and recent attempts to suggest a focused weekly plan.

Dot by dot

AI build phases for KEP

1

Reviewer AI checklist

Add AI suggestion fields to the review workflow: quality notes, duplicate warning, explanation draft, difficulty suggestion.

2

Server-side AI function

Use a backend/edge function so private AI keys never appear in the browser.

3

Student study coach

Generate study guidance from saved attempts, weak subjects, and available resources.

4

Volunteer assistant

Help volunteers submit cleaner questions and translations before reviewer approval.

Safety and trust

KEP AI rules

  • AI suggestions must be labelled as suggestions.
  • Human review is required before publishing educational content.
  • Do not send sensitive student identity data to AI.
  • Never put secret AI keys in frontend files.
  • Do not claim official Kankor prediction or guaranteed admission.
  • Keep a review log so every published change can be traced.
